About Me

On this page, you can find some information about me.

My name is Simon Kerler and I am studying computer science at the University of Augsburg.

As long as I can remember, I have been interested in computers, especially gaming, but also in programming and creating games. Therefore, I wanted to study computer science early on.

In 2011, I acquired my higher education entrance qualification at Ringeisen Gymnasium Ursberg. In its course, i created my first scientific computer program for the simulation of expansion, reflection and interference of one-dimensional waves.

In the same year, I began my bachelor degree course of computer science at the University of Applied Sciences Augsburg. During the internship semester, I worked as web developer with PHP and JavaScript at gogol publishing. I also participated in an externel project at Linnaeus University, Växjö, Sweden. I finished my studies with my bachelor thesis "Generic n-body simulation engine with GPU support".

To complete my education, I began a master degree course at the University of Augsburg in summer semester 2015. At the moment, I am conducting my master thesis about "Collision-Based Particle Simulations on GPUs for Planetary Exploration Systems" at Deutsches Zentrum für Luft- und Raumfahrt in Oberpfaffenhofen.
